Project 3 · Section 02

Artifacts

Two in-class pieces from this semester. Both are included as-is: unpolished, process-level work that shows where the writing actually starts for me.

Artifact 01

Why I Write?

ENC 2135 · In-class freewrite · May 12, 2026

A short in-class freewrite on why I write. I used a lot of CS analogies because that's genuinely how my brain works: writing as clearing the cache, words as a backup drive, revision as debugging. The exercise pushed me to be honest instead of just formal, and it showed me that voice comes through faster when you stop trying to sound like a writer and just say the thing.

Artifact 02

Rhetorical Giraffe

ENC 2135 · Rhetorical Analysis Worksheet · June 2, 2026

A two-part rhetorical analysis worksheet comparing National Geographic's adult and kids' coverage of the same giraffe rescue story. Analyzing both versions side by side made the concept of audience-driven writing concrete: same event, same facts, completely different genre choices. It's where I started thinking about rhetoric as a set of actual decisions rather than a checklist of terms.
